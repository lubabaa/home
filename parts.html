Parts of the computer

  1. CPU
  2. Mother Board
  3. RAM
  4. Basic Input Output System
  5. CD Drive
  6. Floppy Drive
  7. HDD or Hard Disk Drive
  8. Power Supply
  9. Fan

  1. Central Processing Unit

    The CPU, often just called the processor, is the part that contains the microprocessor. That microprocessor is the heart of all the PC’s operations, and the performance of both then hardware and software rely on the processor’s performance.

  2. Mother Board

    The motherboard is the key circuit board holding the most important processing parts of a computer. It helps them receive power and communicate with one another. The main circuit board inside your PC is its motherboard. All parts, inside and out, connect through the motherboard in some way. Most other components are removable and, therefor, can be replaced without replacing the motherboard.

  3. Random Access Memory

    Even the fastest processor needs a place to store information while it's being processed. It serves as the place where the information you're working with wait until you need to pick up and use them. Both a fast CPU and an significant amount of RAM are necessary for a fast PC. Each PC has a maximum amount of RAM it can handle, and slots on the motherboard tell you the type of RAM the PC requires.

  4. Basic Input Output System

    The BIOS software has a number of different roles, but its most important role is to load the operating system. When you turn on your computer and the microprocessor tries to execute its first instruction, it has to get that instruction from somewhere. It cannot get it from the operating system because the operating system is located on a hard disk, and the microprocessor cannot get to it without some instructions that tell it how. The BIOS provides those instructions.

  5. CD Drive

    A CD Drive, also known as an optical disk drive, is a piece of computer hardware that changes data on optical discs using lasers. Optical drives edit, delete and add content to CDs, DVDs and Blu-ray discs in computers and other entertainment electronics.

  6. Floppy Drive

    A floppy disk drive (FDD), or floppy drive, is a hardware device that reads data storage information. Using a floppy disk to exchange data between PCs was a standard method for many computer technicians. The floppy disk was one of the most common ways to store adequate amounts of data outside of a computer’s hard drive for personal use because they were inexpensive and easy to carry. While still a component of some existing computers, floppy drives are essentially obsolete, replaced by inexpensive flash drives and other portable media drives.

  7. Hard Disk Drive

    A hard disk drive (sometimes abbreviated as Hard drive, HD, or HDD) is a memory hardware device that permanently stores and retrieves data on a computer. A hard drive consists of one or more platters where data is written using a magnetic head, all inside of an air-sealed casing. A hard drive can be used to store any data, including pictures, music, videos, text documents, and any files created or downloaded. Also, hard drives store files for the operating system and software programs that run on the computer.

  8. Power Supply

    Almost every component in your PC relies on its power supply. The power supply connects to some type of power source, whether that's a battery in the case of mobile computers, or a power outlet in the case of desktop PCs.

  9. Fan

    Processors, graphics cards, RAM and other parts in computers have increased in speed and power consumption, the more your computer processes, the more heat it generates. The CPU and other components can handle a certain amount of heat. However, if a PC isn't cooled properly, it can overheat, causing costly damage to its components and circuitry. Fans are the most common device used to cool a PC.
